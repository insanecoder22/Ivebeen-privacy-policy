Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 15-11-2025

Thank you for using I've been! This Privacy Policy explains how your data is collected, used, and protected when you use the app.

1. Information We Collect

I've been collects only the information necessary for core app functionality. This includes:

  • Your email address (if you create an account)
  • Your list of visited countries
  • Optional settings such as theme preferences
  • Optional icons/tags added to countries

2. How Data Is Stored

Your data is stored in two places:

  • On your device – visited countries, settings, achievements (local storage)
  • In Firebase Cloud Firestore – your saved countries, achievements, and premium status are synced so you can access them on any device. All cloud data is stored on Firestore servers located in Europe.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your data only for saving and syncing visited countries, managing achievements, enabling premium features, and providing basic app functionality.

We do NOT sell your data, share it with third parties (other than Firebase services used to operate the app), or use it for advertising.

4. Third-Party Services

I've been uses Firebase, a service provided by Google LLC, for authentication (logging in) and Cloud Firestore (saving data). Firebase may collect device information and other diagnostics as described in their own Privacy Policy.

5. Data Security

We rely on Firebase’s built-in security features to protect your data. We do not store any passwords—authentication is handled entirely by Firebase.

6. Your Data Rights

You have the right to request deletion of your account and all stored data. You can do this from the Settings screen inside the app.

7. Children’s Privacy

I've been is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under this age.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When changes are made, you may be prompted to accept the updated version.
